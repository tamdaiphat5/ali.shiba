Soccer in the USA

The Growth of the World's Game in America

Overview

Soccer, known as "football" in most of the world, has seen rapid growth in the United States over the past few decades. With the rise of Major League Soccer (MLS), increased youth participation, and international competitions held on American soil, the sport is now deeply embedded in American culture.

Major League Soccer (MLS)

Founded in 1993, MLS is the top professional soccer league in the United States. It features 29 teams from across the country and Canada. Teams like LA Galaxy, Inter Miami CF, and Atlanta United have passionate fanbases and international stars.

U.S. National Teams

The U.S. Men's and Women's National Teams (USMNT and USWNT) represent the country in international tournaments. The USWNT has won multiple FIFA Women's World Cups and Olympic gold medals, while the men's team continues to rise in international standings.

2026 FIFA World Cup

The USA will co-host the 2026 FIFA World Cup along with Canada and Mexico. It will be one of the biggest sporting events in history, expected to draw millions of fans from around the world.

  • Host Cities: New York, Los Angeles, Dallas, Miami, and more
  • First World Cup with 48 teams
  • Massive investment in stadiums and soccer infrastructure

Conclusion

Soccer is no longer a "foreign" sport in America. With growing interest, talented players, and international events, it continues to establish itself as one of the top sports in the nation.
